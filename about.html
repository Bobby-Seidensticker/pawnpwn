Welcome to Pawn Pwn!

Pawn Pwn is a fun game you can play between two players - either in person, or over the Internet. The game is designed to work well in a web browser, an iPad or any mobile smart phone. Depending on the size of the screen, you will see:

  • A 9 x 9 board on which you will take turns moving your pieces.
  • A graphical representation of the history of the game. The closer your get to your opponent's home row, the lower your colored line gets on the graph. First one to zero wins!
  • A move list. Move notation indicates the row and column your pawn is moving to (just as in chess notation). Wall placements list the square at the upper right of the placed wall, and whether a wall is placed vertically or horizontally.
  • Undo/redo buttons. You can take back the last move, or replay moves that have been taken back by hidding the foward or backward arrows on the board.

Rules for Pawn Pwn

The goal of Pawn Pwn is to get your pawn to your opponent's home row before he can reach yours. On your turn you can either:

  • Move your pawn one space forward, back, left or right.
  • Place one of your walls on the board to make your opponent's path longer.

You are not allowed to completely prevent your opponent from reaching your home row along some path.

Note: when two pawns meet in the middle of the board, your pawn may jump to any square adjacent to your opponent's pawn (i.e., you can jump over your opponent's pawn).

Playing over the Internet

To start a new game with a with an friend over the web, make sure you are signed in to Pageforest, and make your first move. Your game will be automatically saved and the link in your browser address bar will be updated.

After your game is saved, click the About button to come back to this page to send them an email link to your game.
